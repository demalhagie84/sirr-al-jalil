If you have ever lain awake over a debt, a rival, a verdict, a door that will not open — this book was written for you, seven centuries ago, about one sentence.

One sentence

Said twice, at the edge of ruin

Ibrāhīm said it as he was thrown into the fire. The Prophet ﷺ and his companions said it, wounded, marching out to meet an army — ﴾those to whom the people said: they have gathered against you, so fear them﴿ — and it only increased them in faith (Āl ʿImrān 173). Both times the fire cooled and the enemy scattered. This treatise is a seven-hundred-year meditation on why.

The letters

Nineteen letters

Strip the vowel marks and count what remains: nineteen — the number of letters in the Basmala, and a number the Qurʾān itself singles out: ﴿عَلَيْهَا تِسْعَةَ عَشَرَ﴾. Hover any letter to see the value it carries.

The reckoning

They sum to 450

Give each letter its abjad value and add them: 450, exactly. Every square on this page — rows, columns, diagonals — is built to close on this one number, and your browser re-proves each of them every time the page loads.

The trinity

450 breaks into three 150s

Chapter 5's stated secret: the number divides into three equal parts, and each part is itself a word. ʿAlīm — to know. Sulṭān — to stand with authority. Sayf — to cut through. «Behind this number is an amazing secret suggesting a sultan and a sword.»

The first page

And none of it is required

Here is what most readers rush past: before a single square is drawn, the book's first page says the verse works with no timing, no instrument, no condition — an unsheathed sword. The arithmetic is commentary. The secret is tawakkul — the turned heart. Kept with presence, Bāb 3 says, it

The Science of Letters — element, nature, adab

A strand the wider tradition of ʿilm al-ḥurūf shares with this treatise, carried also in Shams al-Maʿārif and kindred works: each letter carries one of the four elements and a paired nature — the same four courses the Forge above lets you turn a square through. Kept here for study, not for show.

الآداب — conditions the masters set before opening any of this
The Sealed Chamber — what this edition leaves closed, deliberately

Roughly a third of the original is sword-work: the seven ways against oppressors, destruction, division, tongue-tying, and coercive taskhīr of named persons. This edition studies that material's arithmetic and withholds its operations — following the book's own fences: «اعلم أن الله سبحانه وتعالى غيور على خلقه» — Allah is jealous over His creation; «فإن من قتل بدعوته كان كمن قتل بسيفه» — whoever kills by his invocation is as one who kills by his sword; «وأن تعفوا أقرب للتقوى» — and that you pardon is nearer to taqwā. The Shādhilī path's greatest karāma is istiqāma. The sword stays in the scabbard; the sufficiency is the whole point.

 
الأربعون

The Path of Forty

the treatise's own daily program, walked one day at a time · everything stays on your device

Forty days is the tradition's unit of transformation — the khalwa, the arbaʿīniyya. The counts here ascend gently toward the book's own prescription — the verse 450 by day and night — and the final ten days seal each recitation with the sixfold fanqalabū and the single riḍwān, exactly as Bāb 1 closes the foundation. Nothing on this path is operative sword-work; it is the licit daily program of the treatise, nothing more and nothing less. If you miss a day, the day simply stays open — return and continue.
